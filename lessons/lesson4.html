Photomasks and Reticles

Photomasks and Reticles
Photomasks, also known as masks or reticles, are essential components in photolithography. They contain patterns that are transferred onto the wafer during the exposure process. Step 1: Design and Fabrication
Photomasks are designed using specialized software. The patterns are then transferred onto a glass substrate coated with a chrome layer through a process called mask fabrication. Step 2: Alignment and Registration
During the lithography process, the photomask is aligned and registered precisely with the wafer to ensure accurate pattern transfer. Step 3: Exposure
The photomask is placed in the exposure system, and light shines through it onto the wafer, transferring the pattern onto the photoresist. Step 4: Reticles in Advanced Lithography
In advanced lithography techniques such as multi-patterning, multiple reticles are used to create complex patterns. Applications
Photomasks are used extensively in the semiconductor industry for manufacturing integrated circuits and microelectronic devices. Note: The animation shown here is a simplified representation for educational purposes.
