Lesson 7: Developing and Etching Processes

Introduction to Developing and Etching

Developing and etching are critical steps in the photolithography process for transferring patterns onto substrates. Developing removes the exposed or unexposed photoresist, depending on the type, while etching selectively removes material from the substrate.

Developing Process

In positive photoresist patterning, the exposed areas become more soluble and are removed by a developer solution, revealing the pattern. In negative photoresist, the unexposed areas are dissolved away, leaving the desired pattern intact.

Etching Techniques

Etching is used to transfer the pattern into the substrate material. There are two main types: wet etching and dry etching. Wet etching involves using a chemical solution to selectively remove material, while dry etching uses plasma to achieve a more controlled and precise etching process.
