TLDR: we explore exposure systems, light sources, and their importance

Exposure Systems and Light Sources

Exposure systems are critical in photolithography, using various light sources to expose patterns on the wafer.

Step 1: Light Sources

Light sources, like lasers and ultraviolet lamps, emit specific wavelengths needed for lithography.

Step 2: Collimation

The light is collimated and directed towards the photomask.

Step 3: Mask Illumination

Collimated light passes through the photomask, containing the pattern to be transferred.

Step 4: Projection and Exposure

The pattern is projected onto the wafer, exposing the photoresist and initiating pattern transfer.

Applications

Exposure systems are crucial in integrated circuit fabrication and advanced microelectronics.
